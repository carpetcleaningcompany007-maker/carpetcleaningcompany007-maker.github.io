Professional rug cleaning

Rugs need a more careful approach than fitted carpet.

Rugs often pick up a surprising amount of dirt, grit, body oils and spills through daily use. Because they are handled differently from fitted carpet, they also need a cleaning approach that takes the material, dyes and construction into account.

A proper rug clean can help improve the appearance of the fibres, freshen the pile and remove the sort of built up soil that makes a rug look tired long before its time.

Whether the rug sits in a hallway, under a dining table or in the middle of the lounge, the aim is to leave it looking cleaner, feeling fresher and better suited to the room again.

How we clean

Cleaning that suits the rug, not a one size fits all job.

Some rugs can take a stronger clean. Others need a gentler method. Good rug cleaning starts with recognising what the rug is made from and what sort of treatment is sensible for it.

1. Assess the rugWe look at the type of rug, fibre, soiling and any marks or areas of concern.
2. Choose the right methodThe clean is matched to the rug rather than assuming every rug can be treated the same way.
3. Clean and extract or freshenThe rug is cleaned using a method suited to its condition so dirt and residues can be removed properly.
4. Final checks and aftercareWe talk you through the finish, drying expectations and any advice for looking after the rug afterwards.
