Floor restoration

Some floors need more than ordinary cleaning to look right again.

When a floor has been heavily used, neglected or allowed to build up layers of grime, ordinary mopping and routine maintenance often stop making much difference. The floor may still look flat, tired and ingrained no matter how often it is cleaned.

Restoration style floor cleaning focuses on lifting the deeper soiling and improving the appearance of the surface so the floor can look fresher, brighter and far more presentable.

This can be useful in homes, commercial properties and practical spaces where the floor has become a major part of the overall visual problem.

How we approach it

The floor type and condition decide the level of work needed.

Good restoration style cleaning starts with understanding what the floor is, how far it has deteriorated and what is realistic. The aim is always to improve the finish in a sensible and safe way for that surface.

1. Assess the floor and its conditionWe look at the material, wear, soil build up and what kind of improvement is realistic.
2. Select the right deep clean approachThe floor is treated with the level of cleaning suited to its type and the condition it is in.
3. Agitate and remove ingrained soilMachine or pad based cleaning is used where appropriate to break through the built up grime.
4. Review the final finishWe talk through the result and any ongoing maintenance advice that will help keep the floor looking better.
